However, when I was 18 months old, I was diagnosed with Coates Disease, a disease that caused the retina in my right eye to dettach, thus causing me to go blind in my right eye. Because of this, I could never play contact sports when I was younger, therefore, I fell in love with the sport of bowling. However, leading up to my college years, I lost that love I had with bowling and now a days, I rarely ever bowl competitively anymore. After reflecting on the 13 years that I bowled competively, as in national tournaments and simple leagues, I finally understand the true reason why I bowled. I bowled because it gave me an opportunity to feel normal, normal as in the ability to play sports since I was never able to play the most basic fundamental sports that most kids got to enjoy.

My Intended Major at UGA

When applying to UGA, I applied to be an intended civil engineering major. However, half way through the summer, I changed my mind and decided I wanted to major in Finance. I decided to change my major because I no longer had any interest in engineering after my advanced Physics class in my senior year of highschool. Yes I still love physics, however, I wouldn't want to do that for the rest of my life. Thus, I had a change in mind and decided that I wanted to do Finance. Math has always came somewhat natural to me and I loved Economics in highschool, thus Finance gives me an alternative to further my studies in both areas. Too, I was really impressed with all I've heard about Terry here at UGA and that definitely aided me in wanting to go the Finance. As of today, I have no idea which route in Finance I want to take, however, I know i will chose the right one!
